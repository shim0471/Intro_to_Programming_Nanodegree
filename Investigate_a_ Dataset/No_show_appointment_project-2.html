def percentage_noshow(data,group_by):
    df = pd.crosstab(index = data[group_by],columns = data.medical_checkup).reset_index()
    df['Percentage'] = (df['show_up']/(df['show_up']+df['missed']))*100
    return df[[group_by,'Percentage']]

5. Conclusion¶

: I analyzed what factors are most important variables for no show. based on this analysis, approximately 20% do not show up on the appointment. And I found that Age, SMS and the number of days between setting the appointment and the actual appointment. In thsi analysis, I call that Waiting day. the longest waiting day is 179 days and median is 11 days. Average of waiting day is 15 days. Particularly, from 0 to 15 days are very high.

In Age variable case, No-show rate is high in teenage group. Particularly, 0-years olds appear to be very high in Show_up data. On the other hand, thirty and forty, seniors are likely to show up.

And other important variabe is 'SMS_received'. this variable is associated with a slightly improvement in show up rate. The patients who receive SMS text message are be likely to attend the appointment than those not receiveing them. Accordingly, I suggest that Hospital send it to patients and it would help doctors avoid no show appointment.

6. Limitation¶

: No-show appointment rate in this data set is from 2016-4-29 to 2016-6-7. I think this period is very short for study to help Hospital. if there are long data for appointment it would be helpful and enhance statistics accuracy.

Also, if there are some useful information like patient's Average income and location, weather conditions. these variables could be good source for analyze No-show rate. For instance, maybe rich people more often visit their doctor. On the other hand, poor meet rarely the doctor. it would be meaningful information for analysis.
